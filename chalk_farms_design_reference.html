About

We build growth engines for companies that can't afford to get it wrong.

Our thesis
Growth marketing is broken for the middle.

The holding companies are built for enterprises. The freelance economy is built for one-off projects. Most performance agencies are built for DTC brands that can pour money into Meta and measure ROAS in real time.

That leaves an entire tier of companies -- growth-stage, $2M to $50M -- either overpaying for an agency that treats them like a B-level client, or piecing together freelancers who don't talk to each other.

Chalk Farms was built for that gap. We bring the accountability of a performance shop and the strategic thinking of an integrated agency -- without the overhead, the layers, or the account managers who've never run a campaign themselves.

How we work

A process built for speed and accountability.

Step 01
Diagnose before we prescribe
Every engagement starts with an audit -- your channels, your funnel, your tracking, your ICP. We don't touch a campaign until we understand what's broken.
Step 02
Build the plan, then the program
Before we launch anything, you have a clear channel strategy, a defined ICP, a creative framework, and a 90-day roadmap.
Step 03
Execute, test, and compound
We launch fast, measure everything, and iterate weekly. Every decision is traceable back to data. The program improves every month.
What we believe

The principles that shape every engagement.

Speed compounds
A good campaign launched today beats a perfect one next month. Momentum matters more than polish.
Creative is a performance lever
The best media buying can't save bad creative. We treat them as one system.
Test everything, assume nothing
Gut instinct gets us to a hypothesis. Data gets us to a decision.
Small is a feature
You work with the people who built the program. No bait-and-switch to junior staff.
Report on outcomes, not activity
Pipeline, CAC, and revenue are the report. Impressions are context.
AI is how we work, not what we sell
We use it to move faster. We don't package it as a product.

The situation

FELFEL had paid media running across three channels. The problem: they couldn't tell what was working. Ads were stale, audiences were broad, attribution was broken, and the program was consistently missing its 40 SQL/month target -- averaging around 30.

The approach

Before touching a single campaign, we rebuilt the tracking infrastructure -- GA4, GTM, and HubSpot. Then redesigned all creative and assigned each channel a distinct role:

LinkedIn
Quality driver. Targets decision-makers by title and company size.
Meta
Volume driver. Expanded audiences for top-of-funnel reach and retargeting.
Google
Efficiency driver. Search and display capturing in-market demand.
